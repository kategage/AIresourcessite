Tool Setup

Claude Code +

Claude Code is an AI agent you run directly from your computer — it can write, edit, research, and automate across files, apps, and the web. These guides and plugins are how to actually get it set up and working well.

💡

Pro tip from experience: Before you start the setup, open a ChatGPT thread and tell it what you're trying to do. When you hit a snag mid-install, you'll have something that already knows your context — and can explain things in plain language in real time.

Build Internal Capacity

Run Your Own AI Workshop

Building AI literacy at your org doesn't require a big budget or outside consultants. A well-run internal workshop demystifies the tools, surfaces your team's best ideas, and builds the champions you need to actually change how you work.

☕

Lunch & Learn

1–2 hours · 10–30 people

Low-barrier intro. One facilitator demos 2–3 tools on real examples from your work. Q&A throughout. Minimal prep, maximum curiosity.

Best for: First-time exposure, leadership buy-in, all-staff intros.

🛠

Half-Day Workshop

3–4 hours · 8–20 people

Small teams tackle real use cases from your work — drafting comms, summarizing reports, building scripts. Debrief together at the end.

Best for: Staff teams, comms departments, organizer cohorts.

⚡

AI Hackathon

6–8 hours · 15–50 people

Teams compete to build AI-powered workflows, tools, or campaigns in a single day. Present and vote at the end. High energy, lasting results.

Best for: Cross-functional teams, digital staff, orgs ready to go deep.

How to run it — 6 steps

  1. 1
    Define your goal. Are you building general literacy, discovering specific use cases, or getting team buy-in? Your goal shapes everything else.
  2. 2
    Pick 2–3 tools to focus on. Don't try to cover the whole landscape. We recommend starting with one general assistant (Claude or ChatGPT) and one specialized tool relevant to your work.
  3. 3
    Design challenges from your real work. Skip generic demos. Use your actual fundraising emails, policy docs, talking points, or volunteer scripts as the raw material.
  4. 4
    Designate a facilitator. Someone who can demo, troubleshoot, and keep energy up. They don't need to be an AI expert — just comfortable enough to explore live.
  5. 5
    Document everything. Prompts that worked, outputs you're proud of, workflows worth repeating. A shared doc is fine. A Notion page is better.
  6. 6
    Commit to one follow-up. Implement one thing within two weeks. Name it, own it, report back. This is how workshops become habits.

Starter prompt ideas for your session

  • "Draft a fundraising email in [org name]'s voice about [current campaign]."
  • "Summarize this policy report in 5 bullet points for a non-expert audience."
  • "Write 5 subject line options for this email. Optimize for open rate."
  • "Turn these talking points into a FAQ for our website."
  • "Write a volunteer recruitment script for someone at a [event type]."
  • "Critique this press release. What's missing? What's unclear?"
